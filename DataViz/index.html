How did COVID-19 affect elections?

The 2020 election, which happened during the COVID-19 pandemic, can bring insights into American voter habits.

By: Alex Taylor

Because of the COVID-19 pandemic, the 2020 election had the lowest percentage of election day votes when compared to the last five elections, according to data from the Maryland State Board of Elections. Instead, it had the highest percentage of mail-in votes.

Despite the election occurring during the middle of the pandemic, the 2020 election had the highest voter turnout out of the last five elections. One reason for this could be that the election happened after the rise in popularity of the Black Lives Matter movement, which could have motivated people to vote. Another reason could be that the election had the potential to replace Donald Trump, the incumbent president who had a very high disapproval rating, according to The American Presidency Project.
